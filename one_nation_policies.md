# Pauline Hanson's One Nation — Signature Policies

**Purpose:** Party-platform source material for the Australian political compass survey. Drawn from One Nation's official site (onenation.org.au, accessed June 2026) and cross-checked against **Pauline Hanson's first-ever National Press Club address, delivered 17 June 2026** (her latest major set-piece speech — confirmed as her only NPC appearance in ~30 years).

> Faithful summary of what the party itself states — descriptive, not endorsing. Figures are One Nation's own claims unless noted; some (e.g. migration totals, the CGT "40,000 young people") are contested and should be verified against an independent fact-check before quoting as fact (see QNews fact-check of the NPC speech).

**Other parties' positions (added 19 Jun 2026):** under each policy below, a *"Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand"* block records each party's stance relative to One Nation's. Tags: **Agree / Partly agree / Mixed / Oppose / No stated policy / Unsettled.** Positions are only stated where reliably known (from the verified bills in `labor_bills_party_positions.md`, established party platforms, or current reporting) or reliably inferable from a long-standing platform; otherwise this is said plainly rather than guessed.

**Leaders as of mid-2026 (post–May 2025 election):** Labor — Anthony Albanese (re-elected); Coalition — **Angus Taylor** (Liberal leader since Feb 2026) with **Matt Canavan** leading the Nationals (since Mar 2026); Greens — **Larissa Waters** (since May 2025, after Adam Bandt lost his seat). Coalition leadership has been volatile since the election.

**⚠️ Major 2026 shift:** In November 2025 the Coalition (Nationals first, then Liberals) **formally dumped the net-zero-by-2050 and 43%-by-2030 targets**, moving sharply toward One Nation on climate/energy. This changes several comparisons below. (Reporting indicates the Coalition still stays *in* the Paris Agreement — verify against a primary source if that distinction is load-bearing.)

## The 10 signature policies

### 1. Slash immigration & population
Cap visas at **130,000/year** (a cut of ~570,000 from current levels), **deport ~75,000 illegal migrants** (overstayers, unlawful residents, foreign criminals), impose an **8-year wait** for citizenship and welfare, abolish student-visa-to-PR pathways, and **withdraw from the UN Refugee Convention**. At the NPC: *"We are a multiracial society, but we must be monocultural,"* and migration should be restricted from nations "immersed in extremism like radical Islam."
*Sources: onenation.org.au/immigration; NPC address 17 Jun 2026.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose.** Backs high (though now falling) managed migration; rejects a 130k cap, mass deportation and leaving the UN Refugee Convention. (It *has* toughened detention/removal of non-citizens — see bills doc — but not migration *levels* to One Nation's degree.)
- **Coalition — Partly agree.** Under Taylor (2026) wants migration cut (a housing-completions-linked NOM cap), ~65,000 overstayer deportations and temporary protection visas — same direction, but no 130k figure and no exit from the Refugee Convention.
- **Greens — Oppose (opposite pole).** Want a higher humanitarian intake, an end to offshore detention and deportations; reject caps and the cultural framing.

### 2. Exit net zero & the Paris Agreement
Reject the net-zero framework and the 43%-by-2030 target entirely; **withdraw from the Paris Agreement**; **end renewable energy subsidies and grants** ("renewable energy bribery"). The party disputes IPCC climate science; at the NPC Hanson called global warming a "hoax."
*Sources: onenation.org.au/net-zero-destroying-australia, /climate; NPC address.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose.** Legislated net zero by 2050 + 43% by 2030 and stays in the Paris Agreement — unchanged in 2026.
- **Coalition — Partly agree (major 2025 reversal).** Formally dumped the net-zero-2050 and 43% targets (Nov 2025) and, under Taylor/Canavan, backs coal and gas — now broadly aligned with One Nation on rejecting the targets. Divergence: reporting says it stays *in* Paris (verify), whereas One Nation wants to exit.
- **Greens — Oppose (opposite pole).** Want faster, deeper cuts and no new coal/gas; the strongest contrast with One Nation.

### 3. Coal, gas & nuclear baseload energy
**Repeal the ban on nuclear power** (one ~1,400 MW reactor, est. $6.8bn) and build new **coal** plants (Collinsville, Port Augusta, Hunter Valley) while extending existing stations; back Australian gas. Goal: cheap, reliable baseload power "at the world's cheapest price."
*Sources: onenation.org.au/embracing-australian-coal-reliable-energy-future, /embracing-nuclear-energy-hydro-australia-future; NPC address.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Mixed.** Backs gas (Future Gas Strategy) and a renewables-plus-storage grid, but **opposes nuclear** (keeps the ban) and opposes new coal expansion. Agrees on gas; opposes the nuclear and new-coal planks.
- **Coalition — Agree.** Pro-coal and pro-gas, and committed to lifting the nuclear ban — the closest major party. (Whether Dutton's 7 government-owned-reactor *build* plan survives under Taylor is unsettled in 2026; the "lift the ban" commitment is firm.)
- **Greens — Oppose (opposite pole).** Anti-coal, anti-gas and strongly anti-nuclear; want renewables.

### 4. Cost-of-living relief
**Cut electricity bills 20%** (via energy-market rule changes favouring coal/gas/nuclear), **halve fuel excise to 26c/litre for 3 years**, raise the **tax-free threshold to $35,000 for self-funded retirees**, let pensioners/veterans earn more before penalty, and lift Medicare rebates.
*Source: onenation.org.au/reduce-cost-of-living.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:** *(all three back cost-of-living relief — the disagreement is the method, not the goal.)*
- **Labor — Mixed.** Delivered energy-bill rebates, income-tax cuts and cheaper childcare/medicines; opposes One Nation's fossil-fuel-driven power-price plan and has not pursued fuel-excise cuts.
- **Coalition — Partly agree.** Favours tax cuts and has used fuel-excise relief before (the 2022 six-month halving); broadly sympathetic to lower-tax/cheaper-fuel measures, but no matching package.
- **Greens — Mixed (oppose the method).** Push relief via dental and mental health into Medicare, rent controls and higher corporate taxes; oppose fuel-excise cuts on climate grounds.

### 5. Family tax (income splitting)
Allow couples with at least one dependent child to **file jointly and split combined income**, so a single-earner household pays less tax — explicitly to make it viable for a parent to stay home rather than use subsidised childcare. (One Nation cites savings of up to ~$9,500/yr for a single earner on $120k.)
*Source: onenation.org.au/famly-tax-policy.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose (reliable inference).** Backs individual taxation plus childcare subsidies; income splitting is long seen by Labor as favouring single-earner, higher-income households.
- **Coalition — No stated policy.** Conservatives have periodically floated family-based taxation, but it is not current Coalition policy — don't assume support.
- **Greens — Oppose (reliable inference).** Favour individual taxation and universal childcare; regard income splitting as regressive and gendered.

### 6. Slash government waste (~$90bn/yr) & shrink the state
Abolish the Department of Climate Change and the National Indigenous Australians Agency, **withdraw from the UN and WHO**, cut foreign aid, and abolish the TGA. At the NPC, Hanson added: **abolish SBS**, convert the **ABC to a city subscription model** (keeping regional funding), and **sack the Human Rights Commission and Sex Discrimination Commissioner**.
*Sources: onenation.org.au/slash-waste; NPC address.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose.** Supports ABC/SBS funding, UN and WHO membership, and the agencies One Nation would abolish (Climate Change dept, NIAA, TGA).
- **Coalition — Partly agree (in impulse, not extent).** Supports spending cuts and has long criticised ABC funding and alleged "bias," but does not back abolishing the public broadcasters or leaving the UN/WHO.
- **Greens — Oppose (opposite pole).** Strongly pro public broadcasting and pro multilateral institutions.

### 7. Restrict foreign ownership of land & assets
Apply a strict **"National Interest" test**, make essential services (power, water, telecoms, ports) **off-limits to foreign investors**, **stop property sales to non-residents/non-citizens**, and **ban further sale of farmland to foreign buyers** (cap leasehold at 25 years; reinstate FIRB review of all agricultural land).
*Sources: onenation.org.au/foreign-ownership, /farming.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:** *(the most bipartisan-friendly One Nation plank — all support some scrutiny, short of blanket bans.)*
- **Labor — Partly agree.** Banned foreign buyers from purchasing established homes (2025–27), tightened FIRB rules and the agricultural-land register — overlaps on property/farmland, but not One Nation's blanket bans.
- **Coalition — Partly agree.** Created the foreign-owned agricultural land register and lowered FIRB farmland thresholds; supports scrutiny, short of outright bans.
- **Greens — Partly agree (different framing).** Support scrutiny of foreign housing/farmland ownership for affordability and environmental reasons, not One Nation's sovereignty framing.

### 8. Free speech & Citizen Initiated Referenda
**Enshrine free speech in the Constitution** and repeal laws/bodies that restrict it; introduce **Citizen Initiated Referenda** so voters can directly propose legislation or referendum questions. (Hanson frames herself as long "silenced" by the political establishment.)
*Sources: onenation.org.au/free-speech-policy, /citizen-initiated-referenda; NPC address.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose.** No support for a constitutional free-speech clause or citizen-initiated referenda.
- **Coalition — No stated policy.** Rhetorically pro-free-speech (e.g. past section 18C debates) but no policy for a constitutional amendment or CIR — don't assume support.
- **Greens — Oppose.** Favour a legislated Human Rights Act / charter approach rather than One Nation's model; do not back CIR.

### 9. Anti-"woke" cultural agenda
Oppose "gender ideology" — at the NPC, Hanson vowed to "dismantle this whole subversive transgender insurgency" and bar transgender women from women's sports/spaces. Return schools to "reading, writing, arithmetic and discipline," reject "white/gender guilt" curricula, and restrict abortion (pro-life). Also: support licensed firearm owners and repeal recent gun restrictions.
*Sources: onenation.org.au/education, /pro-life, /firearms; NPC address.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose.** Supports LGBTQ+ and trans rights, is broadly pro-choice, and backs the National Firearms Agreement (gun control).
- **Coalition — Mixed.** Socially more conservative, with some members sympathetic to restricting trans participation or abortion access — but the party is not committed to One Nation's agenda, and it **supports gun control (the NFA)**, opposing One Nation's firearm liberalisation.
- **Greens — Oppose (opposite pole).** Strongly pro-LGBTQ+/trans rights, pro-choice, and pro gun control.

### 10. Medical freedom & COVID accountability
Oppose **COVID-19 vaccine mandates**, call a **Royal Commission into pandemic management**, and defend individual medical choice. Related health planks: Medicare photo-ID to stop fraud (~$3bn/yr claimed), and HECS forgiveness for doctors/nurses who serve 3 years in the regions.
*Sources: onenation.org.au/covid, /rorts, /health.*

**Where Labor, Coalition & Greens stand:**
- **Labor — Oppose (One Nation framing).** Held a COVID-19 Response Inquiry (2023–24) rather than a royal commission; rejects the anti-vaccine-mandate framing.
- **Coalition — Partly agree (on the inquiry).** Has called for a full royal commission into the COVID response, criticising Labor's inquiry as too narrow — overlaps on the royal-commission demand, less so on "medical freedom"/anti-mandate rhetoric.
- **Greens — Mixed.** Reject One Nation's anti-vaccine framing, but have raised civil-liberties concerns about specific mandates.

## NPC address (17 June 2026) — headline framing
- Slogan: Australians are *"mad as hell"* and *"ready to take their country back."*
- Positioned One Nation as rising past the major parties (media-reported polling — treat as context, not verified).
- New/sharpened commitments vs the website: abolish SBS, ABC-to-subscription, sack the HRC and Sex Discrimination commissioners, "end renewable energy bribery," and the transgender-policy push.
- In Q&A she **declined to commit to detailed tax/childcare numbers**, saying she'd consult experts first.
*Transcript: thenightly.com.au (Hanson full NPC speech); analysis: The Conversation (Grattan); fact-check: QNews.*

## Notes for compass design
- One Nation anchors the **right-authoritarian** and **anti-globalist/sovereignty** corner — strongest contrasts with the Greens on immigration, climate, culture, and international institutions (UN/WHO/Paris).
- Several planks (free speech, CIR, anti-establishment "take the country back") are **populist/outsider** signals that align with the major-party-vs-crossbench axis already noted in `labor_bills_party_positions.md`.
- The platform has **thin or absent detail** on defence (no published page/AUKUS stance), foreign affairs (only via "slash waste"), and banking (the old "people's bank" is abandoned) — avoid building compass questions on those for One Nation without further sourcing.
