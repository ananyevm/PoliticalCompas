# Australian Political Compass — Survey Instrument (30 questions)

**Design:** Party-match. Each question scores all four parties on a **−2 … +2** alignment scale (from the party-policy docs and the bills record). The respondent's answer is mapped **Strongly/Somewhat Agree → +1, Neutral → 0, Somewhat/Strongly Disagree → −1** (strongly vs somewhat collapsed for scoring, as specified — but store the 5-point answer for future use). Final result is each party's **% of their achievable points**, so every party's maximum is 100% by construction.

**Answer options (all questions):** Strongly Agree · Somewhat Agree · Neutral · Somewhat Disagree · Strongly Disagree.

**Each question:** sentence 1 = the big idea; sentence 2 = the concrete implementation ("To achieve this, …" — actual party policy); then the known trade-off. The protagonist party (for whom "agree" aligns most strongly) and the source (platform / legislation) are tagged. **Deploy order should be shuffled** — they are grouped by protagonist here only for review.

> Scoring rows are shown for transparency; hide them from respondents.

## Greens-protagonist

### Q1 · Greens · platform · Climate
Tackling climate change should override other economic concerns, and government must force the transition off fossil fuels. To achieve this, no new coal or gas projects should be approved, emissions should be cut 75% by 2030, a levy should phase out coal exports, and the grid should shift rapidly to 100% renewables.
**Known trade-off:** critics warn this would raise energy prices in the short term and put regional mining and export jobs at risk.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −2*

### Q2 · Greens · platform · Tax & economy
Billionaires and big corporations should pay much more so everyone can have strong public services. To achieve this, billionaires should pay a 10% annual tax on their wealth and the largest companies a 40% extra tax on their biggest profits, funding services like dental into Medicare and free education.
**Known trade-off:** opponents argue very high taxes drive investment and wealthy individuals offshore and can slow the economy.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation +1*

### Q3 · Greens · platform · Immigration & refugees
Australia has a humanitarian duty to people seeking asylum, even at some cost to border control. To achieve this, holding asylum seekers in detention centres in other countries should be ended, the refugee intake raised to 50,000 a year, and permanent visas given to people found to be refugees.
**Known trade-off:** critics say this weakens border control and could restart dangerous boat journeys and people-smuggling.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −2 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −2*

### Q4 · Greens · legislation · Housing
Renters need direct governmental protection from runaway rents. To achieve this, rents should be frozen nationally and then capped, and a government developer should build public and affordable homes.
**Known trade-off:** economists warn rent freezes can shrink rental supply and discourage new building.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −1*

### Q5 · Greens · legislation · Foreign policy & defence
Australia should pursue peace and independence rather than military build-ups. To achieve this, military spending should be cut, and the money redirected to diplomacy and foreign aid.
**Known trade-off:** critics say this would weaken Australia's defences at a time of regional tension.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −2 · Coalition −2 · One Nation 0*

### Q6 · Greens · platform · AUKUS
Australia should not tie itself to a US-led nuclear-submarine build-up. To achieve this, the AUKUS deal to buy nuclear-powered submarines with the US and UK should be cancelled.
**Known trade-off:** critics say cancelling AUKUS would weaken Australia's defences and its alliance with the United States.
*Scoring — Greens +2 · Labor −2 · Coalition −2 · One Nation 0*

## Labor-protagonist

### Q7 · Labor · legislation · Industrial relations
Workers need stronger rights and bargaining power against large employers. To achieve this, unions should be allowed to negotiate pay across several employers at once, casual and agency workers should get equal pay for the same job as permanent staff, workers should gain a right to ignore work calls and messages outside business hours.
**Known trade-off:** business groups say these rules raise costs, reduce flexibility and risk more industrial disputes.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −1*

### Q8 · Labor · platform · Health
Healthcare should be free or near-free when you need it, funded by government. To achieve this, billions should go into Medicare to push bulk-billing (no out-of-pocket cost) toward 90% of GP visits and cap the price of government-subsidised prescription medicines at $25.
**Known trade-off:** it is expensive and adds to the budget — pushing up national debt and taxes — and some argue it should be means-tested.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition 0 · One Nation +1*

### Q9 · Labor · legislation · Housing & tax
Property investor tax breaks should be wound back so first-home buyers aren't outbid by investors. To achieve this, negative gearing should be limited to new builds, so investors can no longer write off rental losses against their wages on established homes.
**Known trade-off:** investors and some economists warn this could reduce rental supply or push up rents.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +2 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −1*

### Q10 · Labor · legislation · Capital gains tax
Taxing labour income at a higher rate than investment income is unfair. To rectify this, the 50% capital-gains tax discount should be scrapped and replaced with a flat 30% minimum tax on investment profits, applied across all asset types, with limited carve-outs for small businesses.
**Known trade-off:** investors and some economists warn a broad minimum tax on gains could discourage investment and risk-taking, while the small-business carve-outs add complexity and loopholes.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +2 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −1*

### Q11 · Labor · legislation · Industry & climate
Government should actively back new clean-energy industries rather than leave it to the market. To achieve this, new clean-energy industries like hydrogen and battery minerals should be subsidised by the government.
**Known trade-off:** critics call it "corporate welfare" that bets taxpayer money on industries that may not succeed.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −2*

### Q12 · Labor · platform · Education
Education and training should be affordable so cost isn't a barrier to getting ahead. To achieve this, all student loan debt should be cut by 20%, people should start repaying at a higher income, and public vocational and trade colleges moved toward being free.
**Known trade-off:** critics call across-the-board debt relief a "reverse Robin Hood" that is unfair to people who never went to university and adds to the budget.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition −1 · One Nation −1*

### Q13 · Labor · platform · Childcare & family
Government should make childcare affordable so cost isn't a barrier to parents working. To achieve this, every family should be guaranteed three days of subsidised childcare and the rule that ties subsidies to how much parents work should be scrapped, as a step toward universal childcare.
**Known trade-off:** critics say it is costly and does little for families where a parent stays home.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition −1 · One Nation −2*

### Q14 · Labor · legislation · Online misinformation
Social-media companies should be made to curb harmful misinformation on their platforms. To achieve this, the media regulator should be given power to make platforms manage seriously harmful misinformation and disinformation.
**Known trade-off:** free-speech advocates warn this hands government and regulators too much say over what people can post online and risks suppressing legitimate debate.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens +1 · Coalition −2 · One Nation −2*

### Q15 · Labor · legislation · Online safety
Children under 16 should be kept off social media to protect them from its harms. To achieve this, platforms should be required to take reasonable steps to stop under-16s holding accounts, with heavy fines for those that don't.
**Known trade-off:** critics warn age checks for everyone raise privacy and surveillance risks, are easy to dodge, and cut young people off from support and community online.
*Scoring — Labor +2 · Greens −1 · Coalition +2 · One Nation −2*

## Coalition-protagonist

### Q16 · Coalition · legislation · Borders & security
Australia must keep firm control of its borders, even if that means tough measures for asylum seekers. To achieve this, asylum seekers should still be held in detention centres in other countries, alongside turning back asylum-seeker boats at sea and detaining and deporting people without valid visas.
**Known trade-off:** human-rights advocates say this causes serious harm to vulnerable people and breaches Australia's obligations.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +2 · Labor +1 · Greens −2*

### Q17 · Coalition · platform · Tax (bracket creep)
People shouldn't pay more tax each year just because inflation pushes them into higher brackets. To achieve this, income-tax thresholds should be permanently indexed to inflation to end "bracket creep" (inflation quietly pushing people into higher tax brackets).
**Known trade-off:** locking in indexation drains government revenue over time and, critics argue, gives the biggest dollar gains to higher earners.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +1 · Labor −1 · Greens −2*

### Q18 · Coalition · platform · Smaller government
The tax system must be favourable to small business. To achieve this, small businesses should be able to immediately deduct the cost of equipment (up to $50,000) from their taxable income, and government spending should be cut.
**Known trade-off:** spending cuts can fall on public services or grow deficits, and critics say instant write-offs mainly reward spending businesses would do anyway.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +2 · Labor −1 · Greens −2*

### Q19 · Coalition · platform · Public debt
Resource-boom windfalls should be saved to pay down government debt rather than spent. To achieve this, a dedicated national fund should bank mining and resource windfalls to reduce public debt for future generations.
**Known trade-off:** critics say locking money away for debt reduction starves public services and infrastructure of funds that could be used now.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +1 · Labor 0 · Greens 0*

### Q20 · Coalition · platform · Energy
Cheap, reliable power matters more than meeting emissions targets. To follow this principle, the targets to cut emissions to net zero by 2050 and sharply by 2030 should be scrapped, and coal and gas kept running.
**Known trade-off:** this would slow climate action and, critics argue, leave Australia behind in the global shift to renewables.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +2 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

### Q21 · Coalition · platform · Nuclear power
Australia should add nuclear power to the grid for reliable, low-emissions power around the clock. To achieve this, the long-standing federal ban on nuclear energy should be lifted and nuclear plants built.
**Known trade-off:** critics say nuclear is the slowest and most expensive option and would take decades, crowding out cheaper renewables.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +2 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

### Q22 · Coalition · platform · Housing
First-home buyers should be able to use their own savings — including super — to crack the market. To achieve this, first-home buyers should be allowed to withdraw up to $50,000 of their superannuation (retirement savings) to put toward a deposit.
**Known trade-off:** economists warn that letting buyers tap super mainly bids up house prices and leaves people with less in retirement.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation 0 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

### Q23 · Coalition · legislation · Industrial relations
Current workplace laws have tilted too far toward unions and should be wound back. To achieve this, the right to ignore work calls and messages outside business hours should be scrapped, pay deals covering several employers at once rolled back, and equal pay for casual and agency workers unwound.
**Known trade-off:** unions warn this strips away protections and bargaining power that lift wages and job security.
*Scoring — Coalition +2 · One Nation +1 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

## One Nation-protagonist

### Q24 · One Nation · platform · Immigration
Immigration is far too high and must be cut dramatically to relieve pressure on housing and services. To achieve this, visas should be capped at around 130,000 a year — Australia currently has no overall visa cap, only a permanent-migration limit of about 185,000.
**Known trade-off:** business and economists warn deep cuts worsen skills shortages and slow the economy.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition +1 · Labor −1 · Greens −2*

### Q25 · One Nation · platform · Gender & identity
Schools and sporting bodies should stop treating trans women as women and trans men as men. To achieve this, gender-identity theory should be kept out of the classroom and transgender women barred from women's sport and single-sex spaces.
**Known trade-off:** critics say this marginalises trans people and overstates how much "gender ideology" is actually taught.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition 0 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

### Q26 · One Nation · platform · Indigenous recognition
Australia should treat everyone the same rather than create special bodies or recognition for Indigenous people. To achieve this, Indigenous-specific agencies, programs and forms of recognition should be wound back, and all Australians treated the same in law and policy.
**Known trade-off:** critics say this ignores entrenched First Nations disadvantage and the case for a say over policies that affect them.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition +1 · Labor −2 · Greens −2*

### Q27 · One Nation · platform · COVID & medical freedom
Governments overreached during COVID with vaccine mandates. Individual medical choice regarding vaccination should be protected. To achieve this, vaccine mandates should be opposed, medical freedom defended, and a royal commission (the highest form of public inquiry) held into the pandemic response.
**Known trade-off:** public-health advocates say this anti-mandate framing undermines responses to future pandemics and lends weight to anti-vaccine sentiment.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition +1 · Labor −1 · Greens 0*

### Q28 · One Nation · platform · Free speech
Free speech should be a guaranteed constitutional right, not just a convention politicians can chip away at. To achieve this, free speech should be written into the Constitution as a protected right.
**Known trade-off:** critics say an absolute free-speech guarantee could shield hate speech and defamation, and that speech is better protected by ordinary laws that can be balanced against other harms.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition 0 · Labor −1 · Greens −1*

### Q29 · One Nation · platform · Direct democracy
Voters should be able to put proposed laws directly to a national vote, not just leave lawmaking to politicians. To achieve this, Citizen Initiated Referenda should be introduced, letting citizens trigger a binding national vote on a law or question they put forward.
**Known trade-off:** critics warn citizen-initiated referenda can be captured by well-funded campaigns and used to push through populist or rights-eroding measures.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition 0 · Labor −1 · Greens −1*

### Q30 · One Nation · platform · Family & tax
Couples share their income and living costs, so they should be taxed as one household rather than as two separate individuals. To achieve this, couples should be allowed to combine their incomes and split them evenly for tax, so that two households with the same total income pay the same tax regardless of how it is divided between partners.
**Known trade-off:** critics say income splitting mainly benefits higher-income single-earner households and discourages women's workforce participation.
*Scoring — One Nation +2 · Coalition +1 · Labor −1 · Greens −2*

## Scoring method

1. Map each answer: **Agree (strongly/somewhat) = +1, Neutral = 0, Disagree (strongly/somewhat) = −1.** (Store the original 5-point answer too.)
2. For each party *p* on each question *q*: **contribution = answer × alignment(q, p).**
   (e.g. you *Agree* with Q1 → Greens +2, Labor −1, Coalition −2, One Nation −2; you *Disagree* → signs flip.)
3. **raw_p = Σ contributions** across all 30 questions.
4. **max_p = Σ |alignment(q, p)|** (the most that party could score). Per-party pools:

   | Party | max points (pool) |
   |---|---|
   | Greens | 47 |
   | Labor | 47 |
   | Coalition | 46 |
   | One Nation | 44 |

5. **Match% = (raw_p + max_p) / (2 × max_p) × 100.** This yields 0–100 for every party (50 = neutral), so the maximum is 100% for all four — equal by construction.

### Worked mini-example
Respondent answers only Q1 *Strongly Agree* and Q24 *Strongly Agree*, Neutral on the rest.
- Q1 (Agree): Greens +2, Labor −1, Coalition −2, One Nation −2
- Q24 (Agree): One Nation +2, Coalition +1, Labor −1, Greens −2
- raw: Greens 0, Labor −2, Coalition −1, One Nation 0
- Match%: Greens (0+47)/94 = 50% · Labor (−2+47)/94 = 48% · Coalition (−1+46)/92 = 49% · One Nation (0+44)/88 = 50%
  (Neutral-heavy answers sit near 50%, as expected; the two strong answers slightly pull the pro-migration-cut and pro-climate-action signals.)

## Design notes
- **Bloc-splitters** (so converged parties don't tie): Q3 refugees, Q5 defence spending, Q6 AUKUS, Q4 rent freeze and Q15 the under-16 social-media ban (Labor +2 vs Greens −1) split Greens from Labor; the softer migration *degree* in Q24 (Coalition +1 vs One Nation +2), the culture split (Coalition 0 on gender in Q25, +1 on Indigenous recognition in Q26 vs One Nation +2 on both), CIR in Q29 (Coalition 0 vs One Nation +2), and the under-16 ban in Q15 (Coalition +2 vs One Nation −2) split the Coalition from One Nation.
- **Source mix:** 20 platform / 10 legislation.
- **Acquiescence note:** the trade-off clause counters agree-everything bias; if desired, ~3–4 stems can be re-phrased from the opposing framing for extra balance.
- Alignment values are duplicated in `compass_scoring_matrix.csv` for the app build.
