# Key Bills of the Albanese Labor Government (2022–2025) — Party Positions

**Purpose:** Source material for an Australian federal "political compass" survey. For each major bill introduced by the Labor government since the May 2022 election (and into the second term after May 2025), this records the position of the **Coalition** (Liberal/National), **One Nation**, and **the Greens**, with a one-line reason. Labor is the proponent of all bills listed, so its position is "For" unless noted.

**How to read positions:** `For` / `Against` / `Abstain`. A position marked **\*** is *directionally* well-evidenced (Hansard speeches, party media releases, reporting) but could not be pinned to an exact recorded division — see the per-bill note. This matters most for **One Nation**, which sits only in the Senate, so its "position" is its Senate vote; on bills that passed without a contested Senate division, or where the roll-call could not be retrieved, its vote is flagged.

**Directional nuance is captured deliberately.** A party can oppose a bill *from the left* (e.g. Greens wanting stronger action) or *from the right* (Coalition/One Nation wanting less intervention). For a compass, *why* a party voted is as important as the vote itself — so the reasons below note the direction.

> ⚠️ **Verification caveat for publication:** Directional positions are solidly sourced. Exact senator-by-senator division tallies were *not* machine-extractable (theyvoteforyou.org.au and aph.gov.au block automated fetching). Before publishing, corroborate the flagged (\*) One Nation votes against the Journals of the Senate / Hansard for the relevant dates.

## 1. Climate & Energy

### Climate Change Act 2022 — *Passed Sep 2022*
Legislates a 43% emissions cut by 2030 (on 2005 levels) and net zero by 2050, with annual ministerial climate statements.
- **Coalition:** Against — argued legislating a target was unnecessary "red tape" and economically risky.
- **One Nation:** Against — Sen. Roberts called for the bill's rejection on climate-sceptic / no-cost-benefit grounds.
- **Greens:** For — backed it as a "floor" while pushing (unsuccessfully) for a 75% target and a ban on new coal/gas. *Opposed-from-the-left framing, voted yes.*

### Safeguard Mechanism (Crediting) Amendment Act 2023 — *Passed Mar 2023*
Forces Australia's ~215 biggest industrial emitters to cut emissions ~4.9%/year via declining, tradable baselines.
- **Coalition:** Against — branded it a "carbon tax by another name" that raises costs and threatens industry.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — anti-climate-policy; direction reliable, exact division not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — negotiated hard *from the left*, winning a "hard cap" on absolute emissions and a "pollution trigger" testing new fossil-fuel projects.

### Nature Repair Act 2023 — *Passed Dec 2023*
Creates a national voluntary biodiversity market — landholders earn tradable certificates for restoring/protecting nature.
- **Coalition:** Against — backflipped from initial support, saying Labor's version diverged too far ahead of EPBC reform.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — consistent anti-scheme stance; exact division not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For (after opposing as "greenwashing") — won removal of "market" branding, a ban on using certificates as offsets, and a water-trigger commitment. *Negotiated from the left.*

### Net Zero Economy Authority Act 2024 — *Passed Sep 2024*
Creates a statutory body to manage the transition — retraining coal/gas workers, attracting clean-energy investment.
- **Coalition:** Against — called it a top-down, "Canberra-centric" union wish-list.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — categorically anti-net-zero (has flagged repeal); exact division not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — secured baseline operational funding and stronger worker/community reporting. *From the left.*

### New Vehicle Efficiency Standard Act 2024 — *Passed May 2024*
Australia's first binding fleet-average CO₂ standard for new light vehicles; penalties push cleaner/electric supply.
- **Coalition:** Against — branded it a "ute tax"/family-car tax (HiLux, Ranger).
- **One Nation:** Against\* — ran a high-profile "Is your HiLux next?" anti–ute-tax campaign; exact division not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — but wanted *tougher* standards; their support was traded for an unrelated win (dropping a gas fast-track). *From the left.*

### Treasury Laws Amendment (Energy Price Relief Plan) Act 2022 — *Passed Dec 2022*
Emergency 12-month $12/GJ cap on new wholesale gas contracts + ~$1.5bn (Commonwealth) household/small-business bill relief.
- **Coalition:** Against — opposed price caps as heavy-handed market intervention / sovereign risk.
- **One Nation:** Against — Sen. Roberts spoke against in the Senate; opposed price intervention.
- **Greens:** For — won a ~$1.5bn low-income electrification package; their own 2-year price-freeze-via-windfall-tax amendment was voted down. *From the left.*

### National Reconstruction Fund Corporation Act 2023 — *Passed Mar 2023*
$15bn investment vehicle (loans/guarantees/equity) across seven priority areas including renewables/low-emission tech.
- **Coalition:** Against — called it a "slush fund"; attacked the Labor–Greens exclusion of new coal/gas.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — division exists but party breakdown not retrievable; do not assume.
- **Greens:** For — negotiated the exclusion of new coal/gas/native-forestry from eligibility. *From the left.*

## 2. Industrial Relations & Workplace

> **Pattern:** Coalition opposed every substantive IR bill; One Nation opposed the four Fair Work bills (per union records, direction reliable but exact rolls unconfirmed); Greens voted For all, *from the left*, repeatedly extracting concessions. Labor needed only the Greens + 1–2 crossbenchers, so One Nation's votes were never decisive.

### Fair Work (Secure Jobs, Better Pay) Act 2022 — *Passed Dec 2022*
Introduced multi-employer bargaining, banned pay-secrecy clauses, limited "zombie" agreements.
- **Coalition:** Against — feared multi-employer bargaining drags in small business and raises strike risk.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — joined the crossbench delay push, then voted no; not decisive.
- **Greens:** For — provided decisive Senate votes; framed it as wage-lifting. *From the left.*

### Fair Work (Protecting Worker Entitlements) Act 2023 — *Passed Jun 2023*
Added super to the National Employment Standards, protected migrant-worker entitlements, expanded parental-leave flexibility.
- **Coalition:** Broadly did not oppose — "bipartisan"; their super-overlap amendments were defeated.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — no specific recorded vote located.
- **Greens:** For — backed worker-protection and migrant-worker provisions.

### Fair Work (Closing Loopholes) Act 2023 — *Passed Dec 2023*
Criminalised intentional wage theft; "same job, same pay" for labour-hire; workplace-delegate rights.
- **Coalition:** Against — opposed same-job-same-pay and wage-theft criminalisation as burdens on business; pledged repeal.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — per union record; not decisive.
- **Greens:** For — pushed to add super theft to the criminal wage-theft provisions. *From the left.*

### Fair Work (Closing Loopholes No. 2) Act 2024 — *Passed Feb 2024*
Casual-conversion pathway, fairer employee/contractor test, gig-worker minimum standards, the **right to disconnect**.
- **Coalition:** Against — opposed gig, casual and right-to-disconnect rules; pledged repeal.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — per union record; not decisive.
- **Greens:** For — *authored* the right-to-disconnect amendment (Sen. Barbara Pocock). *From the left.*

### Paid Parental Leave Amendment (Adding Superannuation) Act 2024 — *Passed Oct 2024*
Pays the 12% Super Guarantee on government-funded Paid Parental Leave.
- **Coalition:** For (in principle) — but its alternative (choice of super / extra leave / lump sum) and small-business carve-out were defeated.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — not a Fair Work bill; no reliable vote located.
- **Greens:** For — originally Greens policy; pushed for 52 weeks PPL by 2030. *From the left.*

## 3. Housing, Tax & Cost of Living

> **Pattern (housing):** Greens consistently opposed/delayed *from the left* (rent freezes/caps, negative-gearing & CGT reform, more public housing) before ultimately voting For — winning real concessions only on HAFF. Coalition & One Nation opposed *from the right* (market intervention, foreign investment, stock-market risk).

### Housing Australia Future Fund Act 2023 — *Passed Sep 2023*
$10bn fund whose annual returns (~$500m/yr) finance social & affordable housing.
- **Coalition:** Against — called it a risky "stock-market gamble" that guarantees no homes.
- **One Nation:** Abstain\* — hostile, reported as abstaining on final passage (medium confidence).
- **Greens:** For — after a months-long standoff demanding a rent freeze; won ~$1bn extra direct social housing + a guaranteed $500m/yr minimum. *From the left.*

### Help to Buy Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
National shared-equity scheme — Commonwealth takes up to a 40% stake so buyers need as little as a 2% deposit.
- **Coalition:** Against — opposed government co-ownership of homes; argued it props up prices.
- **One Nation:** Against — Sen. Roberts called it a "dog of a bill" that fails to fix supply.
- **Greens:** For (after ~2-month delay) — demanded negative-gearing/CGT reform & rent caps, won none, "capitulated." *From the left.* (Labor floated it as a double-dissolution trigger.)

### Treasury Laws Amendment (Build to Rent) Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Tax concessions for institutional build-to-rent housing (higher capital-works deduction, halved MIT withholding tax), conditional on affordability rules.
- **Coalition:** Against — objected to concessions for large/foreign institutional investors.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — Hanson opposed on foreign-ownership grounds; exact division not isolated.
- **Greens:** For (reluctantly) — stalled for months for negative-gearing/CGT reform, then waved it through without headline wins. *From the left.*

### Treasury Laws Amendment (Cost of Living Tax Cuts) Act 2024 — *Passed Mar 2024*
Reworked the legislated Stage 3 income-tax cuts to redistribute relief toward low/middle earners.
- **Coalition:** For — branded Albanese a liar over the broken promise but would not block tax cuts.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — passed broadly with Coalition support; no isolated ON vote confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — welcomed the redistribution but said it didn't go far enough (amendment for more, defeated). *From the left.*

### Family Assistance (Cheaper Child Care) Act 2022 — *Passed Nov 2022*
Lifted the maximum Child Care Subsidy to 90% for families up to $80k, tapering out at $530k.
- **Coalition:** For / did not oppose — supported cheaper childcare in principle. *(Do not confuse with the 2025 Three-Day Guarantee bill, which the Coalition opposed.)*
- **One Nation:** Partial opposition\* — Roberts forced a committee-stage division against part of the bill; no clean final vote pinned.
- **Greens:** For — called it "a step forward"; failed to abolish the "activity test." *From the left.*

### Universities Accord (Student Support and Other Measures) Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Caps HELP/HECS indexation at the lower of CPI or wage growth, backdated to mid-2023.
- **Coalition:** For (with reservations) — "would not stand in the way."
- **One Nation:** Unverified.
- **Greens:** For — moved a (defeated) free-university amendment. *From the left.*

### Universities Accord (Cutting Student Debt by 20 Per Cent) Act 2025 — *Passed Aug 2025*
One-off 20% cut to all student loan balances (~$16bn wiped) and a higher repayment threshold (~$67k).
- **Coalition:** Against (in substance) but did not oppose passage — called it "reverse Robin Hood"; its 3%-indexation alternative was rejected.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — Hanson called debt write-offs "ridiculous"; exact vote not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — backed it as a step toward wiping all student debt. *From the left.*

## 4. Integrity, Democracy & Electoral

### National Anti-Corruption Commission Act 2022 — *Passed Nov 2022*
Establishes an independent federal anti-corruption body able to investigate serious/systemic corruption; public hearings only in "exceptional circumstances."
- **Coalition:** For — bipartisan passage; backed the restrictive public-hearing test.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — Hanson opposed a federal ICAC on principle (cited the Berejiklian case); exact final-passage vote not pinned.
- **Greens:** For — wanted public hearings by default (lost); won stronger Inspector oversight powers. *From the left.*

### The Voice to Parliament Referendum 2023 — *Failed 14 Oct 2023 (No 60% / Yes 40%)*
Proposed constitutional recognition of First Peoples plus an advisory body to Parliament. Positions = Yes/No campaign stance.
- **Coalition:** No (official) — Liberals (Dutton) and Nationals campaigned No; **internal split** — Liberal Julian Leeser resigned the frontbench to campaign Yes; some state branches allowed conscience votes.
- **One Nation:** No — campaigned heavily for No; argued it divided Australians by race.
- **Greens:** Yes — all federal Greens campaigned Yes (after internal Treaty-first debate).

### Electoral Legislation Amendment (Electoral Reform) Act 2025 — *Passed Feb 2025*
Donation cap ($50k), spending caps ($800k/seat; $90m national), lower disclosure threshold ($5k), higher per-vote public funding.
- **Coalition:** For — **did a deal with Labor** (higher caps, $5k threshold) in exchange for support. *This is the cluster's notable major-party alignment.*
- **One Nation:** Against\* — part of the unified crossbench opposition; argued it entrenches the major parties.
- **Greens:** Against — supported the transparency elements but voted no, calling it a major-party "stitch-up" that entrenches the two-party system. *Opposed from the left, against the majors.*

> **Also note — Truth in Political Advertising** (Electoral Communications Bill 2024): Labor's bill **lapsed/never passed**; Coalition effectively unsupportive (free-speech grounds), Greens supportive in principle. No final votes — included for completeness, not as a voted bill.

## 5. Social Policy, Health & Care

### Online Safety Amendment (Social Media Minimum Age) Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Requires platforms to take "reasonable steps" to stop under-16s holding accounts (penalties up to ~$49.5m).
- **Coalition:** For — backed it on child-protection grounds (a few defectors: Archer, Antic, Canavan). *Rare Labor–Coalition alignment.*
- **One Nation:** Against\* — called it government overreach undermining parental responsibility; with the whole crossbench against.
- **Greens:** Against — opposed the rushed process, privacy/age-verification risks, harm to young people's engagement.

### Communications (Combatting Misinformation and Disinformation) Bill 2024 — *Withdrawn Nov 2024 (no Senate vote)*
Would have empowered ACMA to make platforms manage seriously harmful misinformation.
- **Coalition:** Against — called it "dystopian" censorship / a free-speech threat.
- **One Nation:** Against — Roberts & Hanson moved to "kill" the bill; opposed as censorship.
- **Greens:** Against — but for *opposite* reasons: too weak, exempted mainstream media, vague; wanted a "duty of care" model instead.

### Aged Care Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Replaces the 1997 Act with a rights-based framework (Statement of Rights, Support at Home, means-tested co-contributions); implements the Royal Commission.
- **Coalition:** For — genuine bipartisan support after negotiating amendments.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — division table not retrievable; do not assume.
- **Greens:** Against — opposed after Labor sided with the Coalition to reject Greens amendments (wanted criminal penalties for failing providers, no higher co-contributions). *From the left.*

### NDIS Amendment (Getting the NDIS Back on Track No. 1) Act 2024 — *Passed Aug 2024*
Reforms the NDIS to contain cost growth — redefines how supports/budgets are set, frames "foundational supports," tightens access.
- **Coalition:** For (effectively decisive) — backed the amended bill after seeking a longer inquiry.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — pushed a (defeated) fraud inquiry; final vote not confirmed.
- **Greens:** Against — filed a dissenting report; argued it cuts participants' "choice and control." *From the left.*

### Treasury Laws Amendment (Reserve Bank Reforms) Act 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Splits the RBA board into a Monetary Policy Board and a Governance Board; implements the 2023 RBA Review.
- **Coalition:** Against — feared the Treasurer could "stack" the new boards. *Notable: whole Coalition against.*
- **One Nation:** Unverified — not pivotal (passed via Labor + Greens + Pocock + Lambie).
- **Greens:** For — initially blocked it demanding rate cuts, then backed the reforms, giving Labor the numbers.

### Therapeutic Goods (Vaping Reforms) Act 2024 — *Passed Jun 2024*
Bans recreational/disposable vapes; after a Greens deal, adults buy regulated nicotine vapes from pharmacies (Schedule 3) without a prescription.
- **Coalition:** Opposed in principle but did **not** block final passage (wanted over-the-counter adult sales).
- **One Nation:** Against\* — pro-vaping/anti-prohibition (Hanson, Roberts); wanted adult retail like cigarettes.
- **Greens:** For — pivotal negotiator; won the pharmacy model **and decriminalisation of personal-use possession**. *From the left.*

## 6. Immigration, National Security & Industry

> **Pattern (immigration/detention):** Labor and the Coalition repeatedly voted *together* (Coalition pushing Labor to go *harder*), while the Greens opposed from a civil-liberties / refugee-rights angle. One Nation's specific Senate votes were largely unverifiable.

### Migration Amendment (Bridging Visa Conditions) Act 2023 + companion — *Passed Nov–Dec 2023*
Post-NZYQ response: visa conditions for released detainees (ankle monitors, curfews) plus a preventative-detention scheme.
- **Coalition:** For the substance / pushed *harder* — forced the addition of preventative detention; House voting was tactically mixed.
- **One Nation:** Unverified — no recorded party-level Senate division located; pro-detention stance assumed but not confirmed.
- **Greens:** Against — opposed ankle monitors/curfews and "future-crime" preventative detention.

### Migration Amendment (Removal and Other Measures) Bill 2024 — *Passed Nov 2024*
Powers to direct non-citizens to cooperate with their own deportation (mandatory minimum jail for non-cooperation) and "third-country" removal arrangements.
- **Coalition:** For — backed expanded removal/third-country powers (consistent with pushing harder).
- **One Nation:** Unverified — tough-border framing assumed; no recorded vote confirmed.
- **Greens:** Against — condemned it as punitive, criminalising, a threat to non-refoulement (Shoebridge dissent). *From the left.*

### November 2024 Migration Package (Migration Amendment Act 2024 + Prohibiting Items in Detention Act 2024) — *Passed Nov 2024*
Expands powers to re-detain/remove and bans phones/items in detention facilities.
- **Coalition:** For — voted with Labor; "nobody rebelled." *Clearest Labor–Coalition alignment.*
- **One Nation:** Unverified.
- **Greens:** Against — civil-liberties/refugee-rights opposition; condemned the haste.

### Digital ID Act 2024 — *Passed Mar–May 2024*
National accreditation/governance framework for digital identity, extended to states and the private sector.
- **Coalition:** Against — raised privacy/scope-creep and rushed-process objections; sided with One Nation.
- **One Nation:** Against — opposed on privacy/"surveillance" grounds; voted with the Coalition.
- **Greens:** For — after winning amendments making it genuinely voluntary with a non-digital alternative. *(Here Greens sided with Labor against Coalition + One Nation.)*

### Future Made in Australia (Production Tax Credits and Other Measures) Act 2025 — *Passed Feb 2025*
Refundable production tax incentives for renewable hydrogen ($2/kg) and critical-minerals processing (10%). *(Industry × climate.)*
- **Coalition:** Against — attacked the credits as "corporate welfare"/poor-value subsidy.
- **One Nation:** Against\* — opposes green-industry subsidies; exact division not confirmed.
- **Greens:** For — after winning an amendment barring uranium from qualifying as a critical mineral. *From the left.*

### Defence Trade Controls Amendment Act 2024 (AUKUS) — *Passed Mar 2024*
Creates a licence-free defence-trade environment with the US & UK; new criminal offences for unauthorised supply to other countries.
- **Coalition:** For — bipartisan AUKUS support.
- **One Nation:** Unverified.
- **Greens:** Against\* — Shoebridge criticised rushed process, harm to research, new criminal offences; clearly opposed but final division not confirmed.

### Anti-Money Laundering and CTF Amendment Act 2024 ("Tranche 2") — *Passed Nov 2024*
Extends AML/CTF obligations to lawyers, accountants, conveyancers, real-estate agents and precious-metals dealers.
- **Coalition:** Unverified — sought (failed) small-business carve-outs, did not block; clean for/against not confirmed.
- **One Nation:** Unverified.
- **Greens:** Unverified — no clear recorded division located.

## Cross-cutting axes for compass design

These recurring alignments are the most useful raw material for mapping a respondent onto axes:

1. **Climate/energy left–right (the cleanest axis):** Coalition *Against* (cost/jobs/intervention) → One Nation *Against* (anti-climate) on one side; Greens *For from the left* (wanting more) + Labor in the middle.
2. **Industrial relations:** Identical shape — Labor + Greens vs Coalition + One Nation.
3. **Housing/economic intervention:** Greens oppose *from the left* (not enough); Coalition/One Nation oppose *from the right* (too much intervention). Good for separating "more vs less government" from "left vs right."
4. **Civil liberties / social-authoritarian axis:** Immigration-detention bills (Labor + Coalition *together*; Greens against) and the social-media ban (Labor + Coalition; Greens + One Nation against) cut *across* the economic axis — ideal for a second (libertarian–authoritarian) dimension.
5. **Major-party vs minor-party:** Electoral reform (Labor + Coalition vs the entire crossbench) is a distinct "establishment vs outsider" signal.
